# Case Study 11: "Thanks, Ballet!" — The Genesis of Brutal Bap

## Objective
Technical audit of the **Foundational Canon of Brutal Bap** and the implementation of the **"Wrong Thing" Paradigm** within the RHA Protocol. This case study documents the synthesis of 90s Boom-Bap, Indian Folk (Tabla/Sitar), and Industrial "Wall of Sound" textures.

## Technical Architecture

* **Genre Engineering:** Brutal Bap / Indian Folk Fusion / Industrial Boom-Bap.
* **Vocal Profile:** * **Lead:** Male Raw Emotional Baritone.
    * **Delivery:** Transition from low-volume, gravelly introspective verses to explosive, shouted "Sovereign Outbursts" in the final choruses.
* **BPM & Dynamics:** * Verse: 90 BPM (Boom-Bap).
    * Pre-Chorus: 85 BPM (Tension).
    * Chorus: 100 BPM (Industrial Assault).
    * Outro: 70 BPM (Resolute Fade).
* **Sonic Blueprint:** Sampled Sitar riffs, live Tabla/Dholak percussion, groovy funky basslines, and massive industrial distorted guitar layers in the finale.

## Biological Integrity Audit

* **Quantization:** 0% (Temporal Friction). Deliberate preservation of unquantized rhythmic stumbles and natural "swing" in Indian percussion.
* **Vocal Processing:** Zero pitch correction. High-decibel choruses preserved with natural vocal breaking and physical strain to maintain **Biological Integrity**.
* **Acoustic Signatures:** Intimate room resonance in verses shifting to a monolithic, saturated acoustic environment in the climax.
